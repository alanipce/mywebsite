Getting hired!

I interviewed for a software development position in the summer of 2014 at the recommendation of Professor Arturo Concepcion. But unfortunately was not offered the job. It was disappointing, but I decided to collaborate with the team unpaid through independent study for the experience. After 3 months of work they decided to hire me due to my strong work ethic, capacity to learn fast, and be a team player. I continued to work with the team until the day I graduated!

Growing Professionally

This was my first job as a software developer where I got to experience the real deal. I had responsibilities, meetings, presentations, project requirements, deadlines, I had to plan and deliver. I worked closely with clients to understand their vision, and wrote up concrete requirements for their expectations, and continued to work with them through the mobile apps lifecycle. I collaborated with other developers and departments to meet the universities standards in accessibility, security, and branding. I presented in front of University leaders across the CSU, assisted students in CSE 455 course, and underwent specialized training. Most importantly I had figure out what I need to know and go out and learn it.

Working with Chancellors office

I was tasked with developing the apps for the Red Folder initiative. Red Folder was a CSU-wide program for assisting students in distress. It was an amazing opportunity to develop the app for the program that would help students out across all 23 CSU campuses.

Making Connections

Being part of the team allowed me to make connections. Two important connections I made was with Michael Edwards, the CEO of CaseAide, and the TuMee team. I would go on to work with Michael on CaseAide and TuMee after graduating.

Red Folder

Red Folder is a reference tool that contains safety tips and contact information for a wide variety of emergency campus resources. It also provides guidance on responding to incidents in which a community member might report being a danger to self or a threat to others.
